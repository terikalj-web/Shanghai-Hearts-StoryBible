- Easy smile

---

## Presence

Ren attracts attention effortlessly.

Not because he is loud.

Not because he demands it.

Because there is something slightly dangerous about him.

The tattoos hidden beneath expensive suits.

The old scars.

The reputation that follows him into rooms before he arrives.

People are rarely afraid of Ren.

They are afraid of the people who are afraid of Ren.

He smiles easily.

Which somehow makes him more intimidating.

Most people leave interactions with the same thought:

*He was nice.*

*I should probably stay on his good side.*

### Private Reality

Far more dangerous than he appears.

Raised around power.

Raised around violence.

Much lonelier than most people realize.

---

## Relationship With Violence

Ren does not enjoy violence.

He simply accepts it as a reality.

He grew up around men who used it as a tool.

As a result, he is rarely intimidated by threats.

Most people have never seen Ren truly angry.

The few who have usually stop talking about it.

---

## Relationship With Love

Ren falls in love completely.

Not recklessly.

Not casually.

Completely.

Once someone becomes important to him, they remain important long after they should.

---

## Relationship With Lihuan

### First Impression

Beautiful.

Interesting.

Different.

### What He Notices

- Her confidence
- Her independence
- Her kindness
- Her refusal to chase attention
- Her ability to be completely comfortable alone

### What Attracts Him

Lihuan never wanted anything from him.

Not his money.

Not his status.

Not his family.

Not his attention.

She simply liked him.

### Their Relationship

Met during university orientation.

Became best friends.

Spent years together.

Study sessions.

Road trips.

Late-night food runs.

Phone calls at impossible hours.

The friendship became so close that rumors followed them for years.

Most people assumed they were together.

Neither corrected them.

### What She Represents

Home.

Freedom.

Youth.

The happiest years of his life.

## Story Function

Secondary Male Lead.

Represents freedom, friendship, youth, and first love.

Acts as the emotional contrast to Liang Zhan.

Where Zhan represents duty and control, Ren represents connection and choice.

His relationship with Lihuan represents the life she built for herself in America and the future she might have had.
